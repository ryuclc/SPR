Audio samples from "Pronunciation dictionary-free multilingual speech synthesis based on continuous phonetic representations"

Authors: Chang Liu, Zhenhua Ling, Linghui Chen

Abstract: This paper proposes a multilingual speech synthesis method which is based on continuous phonetic representations (CPR) and does not rely on the pronunciation dictionaries of target languages. State-of-the-art speech synthesis systems generally require language-specific pronunciation dictionaries to obtain phoneme sequences and use them as the input of acoustic models. However, it is usually difficult to acquire a large-scale and accurate enough pronunciation dictionary for a new language. In our proposed method, a language-independent automatic speech recognition (LI-ASR) model with a connectionist temporal classification (CTC) loss is first built to extract segment-level CPRs from the audio data of target languages. Then, a CPR-based acoustic model is designed, which first predicts CPRs from texts and then converts CPRs into mel spectrograms. The results of our experiments on six languages show that the proposed method outperformed the baseline method that directly predicted mel spectrograms from character sequences and the method that employed the discrete phonetic symbols recognized by LI-ASR as intermediate representations. Its performance was also better than or comparable with the method that takes phoneme sequences as the input of acoustic models for the six target languages.
